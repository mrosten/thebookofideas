<h4>The Uncertainty Principle of Trust</h4>
<p>God runs the world between two poles: absolute <strong>Trust in God</strong> and <strong>Self-Reliance</strong>. Like the uncertainty principle in physics, the exact point where you belong at any given moment is indeterminate. It is a dynamic tension between the <em>Circular Sephirot</em> (Nature/Elohim) and the <em>Straight Sephirot</em> (Infinite Light).</p>

<h4>Prophecy in the Night</h4>
<p>Prophecy did not end with the Temple; it shifted into the realm of the unconscious. As the Rambam notes, almost all prophets (except Moses) received their messages while asleep. In our era, a dream is often "more than a dream"—it is the voice of the soul speaking when the static of the ego is silenced.</p>
