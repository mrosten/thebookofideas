The

traditional way of looking at the laws of the Torah or morality in general is

that it is some standard rule of conduct, and that theoretically there is some

infinite or finite set of rules for all situations. The Rambam's way is that

there is no external set of rules, only internal rules within each person's

heart and mind that we may be unaware of because of the physicality of the body

that causes us not to hear our minds or heart.

Thus,

there comes a sign to know if someone actually is going to Gan Eden after they

pass away or not. If they have no conscience, i.e., no pangs of guilt when they

cause some type of emotional pain or otherwise to another person, that is a

sign they are completely out of touch with their heart and mind or that their

inner heart and mind have left them and thus they will not go to the light

afterwards. This is the rational

explanation

of the
