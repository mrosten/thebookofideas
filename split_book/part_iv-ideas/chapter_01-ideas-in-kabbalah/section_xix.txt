There are

ten empty spaces, and each is divisible into ten, which are divisible into ten

ad infinitum. Each one is on a different plane, i.e., the three dimensional

sphere is surrounded by a 4-D sphere which is surrounded etc. Each has its own

surrounding sephirot in its own dimension. For instance, our empty space is

surrounded by Royalty of the Infinite One which is surrounded etc. Like this:

The lines

Adam Kadmon, the sephirot in the shape of a person, which is the general

subject of the Eitz Chayim. The time-axis is negative, so to get the regular

order we are used to you have to turn it upside down. This, it seems, is the

root of the Baal Hatanya's idea of equating
