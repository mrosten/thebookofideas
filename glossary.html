Glossary

13 Principles of Faith
Thirteen fundamental principles organized by the Rambam that define Judaism. These principles are derived from the Talmud, but were organized and clarified by the Rambam.
Adam Kadmon
The region above Emanation. It is associated with the crown on top of the Yud of the Divine Name.
Baal Ha Tanya
Rabbi Shneur Zalman of Liadi. A disciple of the Magid of Mezritch.
Baba Sali
Rabbi Israel Abuchatzeira, a Kabbalist of the Twentieth century, great grand-son of Rabbi Shmuel Abuchatzeira, a disciple of Rabbi Chayim Vital.
Banach Space
A normed space that is complete. Any normed space can be made into a Banach space by completing it. "Complete" means every fundamental sequence has a limit; it is like having an outside hull. A linear space is called "normed" if, for every element, there is associated a real number (a distance greater than or equal to zero) that satisfies basic qualities of distance.
Binomial
An algebraic expression consisting of two terms. In this system, it can represent the dual nature of spiritual forces (e.g., Chessed and Gevurah).
Breaking of the Vessels (Shevirat HaKeilim)

It refers to the shattering of the spiritual vessels during the process of creation, which resulted in sparks of holiness being scattered throughout the physical world.
Cantor's diagonal argument
A proof by Georg Cantor that the set of real numbers is uncountable. By taking a list of real numbers between 0 and 1 and changing the diagonal digits, one finds a number that cannot be on the list.
Chasidus (Chasidism)
The mystical and social movement within Orthodox Judaism founded by the Baal Shem Tov, emphasizing joy, faith, and the omnipresence of God.
Chaya
The fourth highest level of the soul, associated with the level of "Life" energy.
Empty Space (Challal HaPanui)
The primordial "spiritual vacuum" created by the Tzimtzum (contraction) of God's infinite light, providing a "place" where finite existence can emerge. As Rabbi Nachman teaches: "Because of the compassion of God, He created the world... Therefore he made everything from the top of emanation to the center of the earth in for this purpose." (Lekutai Moharan).
Guide for the Perplexed (Moreh Nevuchim)
The philosophical masterpiece by the Rambam (Maimonides), which seeks to reconcile Torah with reason and logic.
Henkin Sentence
A sentence in mathematical logic which asserts its own validity. In fact, it is always valid.
Higgs Field
A physical field that, according to the Standard Model, transfers energy into matter, granting particles their mass.
Hilbert space
In mathematics, Hilbert spaces allow generalizing the methods of linear algebra and calculus to spaces that may be infinite-dimensional. Formally, a Hilbert space is a vector space equipped with an inner product that defines distance.
Kabbalah
The esoteric and mystical tradition of the Torah, exploring the nature of God, the soul, and the structure of creation.
Klipat Noga
The outer shell that is a mixture of good and evil. It is associated with the Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil.
Klipot
The "Husks" or "Shells" of impurity which conceal the sparks of holiness. They represent the barrier between man and God.
Lekutai Moharan
The primary collection of teachings by Rabbi Nachman of Breslov, providing a roadmap for spiritual elevation through faith, joy, and prayer.
Metric Space
A set where a notion of distance between elements is defined. The distance between two points is always greater than or equal to zero. This is distinct from a Banach space, where distance is associated with each single element.
Matrix
An array of numbers each representing the value of a variable. In this book, the term refers to spiritual variables and interconnected equations in the structure of creation.
Mitzvot
The 613 commandments of the Torah, serving as the practical application of spiritual laws in the physical realm.
Raisha Delo Isyada (The Unknown Head)
The highest point of the Crown (Keter) of Emanation, representing the level of Divine Will that is beyond human comprehension.
R-L-C Circuit
An electric circuit that contains a resistor (R), an inductor (L), and a capacitor (C).
Rambam (Maimonides)
Rabbi Moses ben Maimon, one of the most prolific and influential Torah scholars, philosophers, and physicians of the Middle Ages.
Sephirot
The ten divine emanations or channels of creative energy through which God interacts with the world. Related to the verse "To You O Lord is the Greatness (Chessed), the Power (Gevurah), and the Beauty (Tiferet)..." (Chronicles I, 29).
Sephiros deYosher (The Straight Sephirot)
The arrangement of sephirot in the "form of a person," as opposed to the circular arrangement, representing a system where spiritual forces can interact and balance one another.
The Shemita Opinion
The Kabbalistic view (referenced by R. Isaac of Akko and R. Abulafia) that the world undergoes cycles of 7,000 years. This perspective can be used to reconcile Torah chronology with the billion-year age of the universe suggested by science.
Schwarzschild Radius
The region of a black hole from which no light can escape.
Tensor
A mathematical object used to describe physical properties, like mass-energy density and momentum, at any point in spacetime. In this work, it is used as a metaphor for spiritual density.
The Alter of Slobodka
Rabbi Nosson Tzvi Finkel, a bridge between the Musar movement and the depth of the human soul.
Super Numbers
Numbers that must exist if we add a sixth axiom to TNT (Typographical Number Theory), i.e., that it is not true that for all \( a, (0+a) = a \). It turns out that with this axiom, we have one axiom that says all natural numbers have a certain property and another which says there are numbers which do not have it. Thus, we have numbers that, if you add zero to them, result in a different value.
Tikunim of Rabbi Moshe Chayim Luzzatto
A Kabbalistic work by the Ramchal, similar in structure and depth to the Tikunei HaZohar attributed to Rabbi Shimon Bar Yochai.
Torah
The Five Books of Moses and the Oral Law which was also handed down to Moses at Mount Sinai.
TNT (Typographical Number Theory)
A formal system, introduced in Douglas Hofstadter's GÃ¶del, Escher, Bach, that represents the natural numbers and their properties.
Yidishkeit
The warmth and spiritual essence of traditional Jewish life and practice.
Yechida
The highest of the five levels of the soul, representing the point of absolute union with the Infinite.
Yud
The tenth letter of the Hebrew alphabet.
Zohar
The major work of Kabbalah, traditionally attributed to Rabbi Shimon Bar Yochai.
