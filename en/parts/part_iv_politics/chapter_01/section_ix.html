Friendships are for instrumentality. Love is another word for usefulness. My only answer in this book is to stick with your family and true friends people that you know have a true love for you. This is worth more than all theology and even true religion and faith put together. I don't think we should go back to the Middle Ages. This advice of mine is to provide true support in the world that is lonely and insecure, and bypass authoritarian systems that will fail. For in the long run Man won't give up his freedom and the all the cults like Fundamentalist Islam or Orthodox Judaism will rise and fall like fascism.

To assume that people act in a certain way irrelevant of the doctrine they follow is simply not true. The laws people believe in affect their character. Statistically speaking if you measure people's prejudice before you pass a law outlawing prejudice and then immediately after you pass the law you measure it again, the amount of prejudice is found to be reduced significantly. So, if the rabbis of the Talmud itself say all problems in the world come from the rabbis of each generation and we see the doctrine they follow is that of the Talmud itself, we know that something is missing in that doctrine that is needed to make people righteous.
