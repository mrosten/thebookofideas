The Alchemy of Love

Human emotions are the most powerful forces building and breaking societies. Of these, Love is the highest of the seven lower Sephirot (forces), yet it remains a "vessel" for the higher intellectual fields. It is the only force capable of bridging the gap between the physical and the spiritual worlds. True love requires the readiness for self-sacrificeâ€”the willingness to give up one's life for another.

The Commitment of Foundation The stability of a marriage depends on the degree of commitment. If one partner is fully committed but the other is only "committed to the perks" of the relationship, the structure will eventually collapse. Commitment provides a protective Makif (Outer Light) that wards off the challenges of time and familiarity.

Divine Presence and the Klipot

When the Divine Presence (Shekhinah) falls into the Klipot (Shadows), marriage can become a source of profound judgment. Many women end up marrying a "shell" of a man, bringing more shadows into the world unless they can free themselves through spiritual elevation. Conversely, a marriage between two righteous individuals creates a "revival" in miniature, allowing them to exist in God's light together forever.

The Secret of the Species The "Genius of the Species" seeks the constant improvement of quality over quantity. Promiscuous sex is a loss of this quality-control; marriage is the sanctification of the sifting process.

The Rule of Cleanliness

Spiritual elevation reflects even in the physical area of cleanliness. However, true cleanliness must come from within. A parent cannot "rule" a teenager into cleanliness; they must wait for the internal disgust of the filth to prompt the action.
