Jesus contains the vessels of Tohu [chaos] and thus majors in physical healing, and in bringing people to the Garden of Eden in the next world; which is also so to speak relevant to the vessels of the light, which are people's souls, and the actual surroundings of those souls in the next world. Rabbi Nachman has to do with more with the drawing of the infinite light of the crown, or of wisdom its vessel. He has very little to do with healing anybody or miracles in general.

We are indebted to the Hindu thinkers [Shankara and Ramanuja] for the emphasis and clarity of their belief that the God is the only reality. This belief eventually got into Judaism, where it was all the time (but submerged), until the Baal Shem Tov brought it to the surface and the Litvaks were forced to concede to its truth - however reluctantly that may have been. Yet the pessimism of Hindu and Buddhist teachings (i.e., their idea that human deeds are fruitless and have no essential value) stifled their civilization for 2000 years while the Jewish belief in the power of the mind [the father] and the Christian belief in the power of good deeds [the mother church] shaped and brought forth western civilization.
