"Nietzsche argued that the Bible guarantees immediate salvation for the righteous. But the Sages offer a far more complex formula‚Äîone that explains why the wicked prosper and the righteous suffer in this world."

The Mathematics of Merit

The universe operates on a system of "majority quality." Each deed, word, and thought contributes to a soul's weight in the balance of justice.

Life on Earth (Temporal)
Purification

Suffering for sins here to arrive pure in the Next World.

Prosperity

Receiving reward for few good deeds here; no root remains for the Next World.

The Next World (Eternal)
Eternal Light

Full reward for a majority of goodness.

Loss

Payment for a majority of sins.

Collective vs. Individual Reward

When the Torah guarantees rain and prosperity for keeping the mitzvot, it is speaking to the Plural‚Äîthe Jewish people as a collective. If 51% of the people are good, the land as a whole prospers. Individually, however, the wicked may still hold the high offices and wealth, while the righteous are preserved in a lowly way until their "horn is raised."

The Three Storehouses of Reward The afterlife is tiered according to the nature of one's service:
  1. Lower Gan Eden: For those whose physical deeds were righteous.
  2. Middle Gan Eden: For those who guarded their speech and spoke words of truth.
  3. Higher Gan Eden: For those whose inner thoughts and feelings were integrated into the Light.

Ultimately, God's justice is not a simple "if-then" statement, but a deep sifting of every level of human consciousness.
