Chapter 9: On Learning Torah ‚Äî Section I

"It is better for one whose root is in learning to sit and draw the light." ‚Äî The Primacy of Torah

The Dynamics of Learning

The Torah places learning on a higher plane than direct action. While deeds create the physical vessels, learning draws the vertical light from the higher worlds. Since all of Israel is spiritually connected, one who is dedicated to learning acts as a generator of light for those whose root is in the world of Action. Even prayer is subordinate to this flow, for while prayer is a call from below, Torah is the response from above.

The Nucleus of Truth

Hebrew contains the Inner Light. It is precise and technical, similar to the source code of reality. However, the "Surrounding Light" of Hebrew was lost and scattered into other languages.

Precision
The Narrative Glow

Other languages, like English, contain the Outer Light. They provide the emotional impact and the overarching story-line that is often lost in technical Hebrew study. "Love your neighbor" feels like a slogan in Hebrew, but a movement in English.

Impact

The Rabbi Nachman Method

There is a unique speed to wisdom. Rabbi Nachman taught that the best way to absorb learning is to speak the words quickly and in sequence, without repeats or interruptions. This taps into the subconscious mind's power of pattern recognition. When learning is approached as a continuous flow‚Äîeven learning "backwards" from the end of a page‚Äîit shatters stagnant spiritual judgments and allows the mind to absorb the "Idea Space" of the text regardless of immediate conscious understanding.

The Integrity of Intention Torah study has power only when the intention is pure service to the Divine. When Torah is used as a tool for status or income‚Äîsuch as qualifying for a professional position to make money‚Äîthe conclusions reached will inevitably be flawed. This is why many "modern judges" inadvertantly contribute to the troubles of the world; their lens is tinted by the need for a certificate rather than a connection.
