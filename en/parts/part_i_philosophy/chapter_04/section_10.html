The Rambam's writes, ‚ÄúConsequently he who wishes to reach perfection, must first study Logic, and then the different parts of Mathematics in their right order, then physics, then Metaphysics (what today we call philosophy) -- [The Guide I:34]. The Crown of Intuition

‚ú® üí° ‚ö°
"What is internalized in the mind becomes part of the spirit."

Meditation accesses the Crown‚Äîthe level above logic‚Äîallowing intuition to flow down into the prepared vessel of the mind.

The Rambam obviously held that there is a point that what is internalized in the mind becomes part of the spirit ‚Äî similar to Hegel. However, I think that by meditation, which corresponds to the level of the Crown, one can increase his degree of intuition and awareness of God's will.

The Rambam wrote in the Guide and Mishna Torah that one needs to learn Torah first in order to know the laws of conduct ‚Äî however, he also writes in the beginning of Mishna Torah that work contains the entire Oral Law and one needs only to learn the First Testament and the Mishna Torah and nothing else. Also, he writes that the Mishna Torah is for the great and the small (i.e., average intelligence). This suffices to answer those who try to avoid the Rambam's point of learning science and philosophy ‚Äî by making the study of the Torah longer and harder than it has to be.

He specifically holds that there was written a code of Jewish law and only for special reasons were non-halachic opinions added, but theoretically, just a simple halachic book was all that was needed. So even if there are opinions not like the Rambam, it would be sufficient to add them to the Mishna Torah, and not spend all one's life learning that which the Rambam held was almost a waste of time. The Rambam isn't alone in this, for there is also the Duties of the Heart that held that going into halachic matters that don't concern one is a complete waste of time and one rather should learn science and philosophy to come to love and fear of God.
