Focal Points and Orbits

Most people's lives have a center of mass.

  • The Basic Level: God alone is the center of gravity.
  • The Practical Level: We often "orbit" around tools or people (Jesus, Rabbi Nachman, Halacha, Israel) to find God.

The right way is to concentrate on God alone, using these "tools" as means to an end, not end-points of focus.

The Wave of Progress

People's growth often follows a Sine Function. High points are followed by lows as we prepare for the next world. Unhappiness often comes from the pursuit of happiness. True Enlightenment (like Jacob's) involves both the promise from God and the struggle with the "Enemy."

The Mountain of Tools

Climbing the mountain of God requires different tools for different people:

  • Musar: Ethics and fear.
  • Chassidus / Kabbalah: Inner secrets.
  • Hisbodedut: Solitude and meditation in the forest.
  • Halacha: The objective law.

Everything depends on the individual's stamina and intelligence. There is no one-size-fits-all doctrine; there are only tools for the soul.

The State of Solitude

Praying alone (or meditating in a closet like Descartes) allows the soul to return to its Wave State. When observed or fixed in community, spiritual energy "collapses" into a particle‚Äîa vessel with specific time and space. Solitude preserves the wave-like connection to the Infinite.
