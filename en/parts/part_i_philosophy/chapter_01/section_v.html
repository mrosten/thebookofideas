Section V ‚Äî The Two Modes of Light

Circular and Straight

The idea that "nothing exists besides God" operates on the level of Sovev Kol Almin‚ÄîHe surrounds all worlds. This is the realm of the Circular Sephirot (Iggulim): God's infinite light that envelops all creation equally, without distinction.

But there is another mode: Memale Kol Almin‚ÄîHe fills all worlds. Here, the Straight Sephirot (Yosher) operate: God's life energy is divided and distributed among the different levels of creation in a hierarchical order.

Sparks and Life Force

Two Types of Divine Presence

  • The Sparks (Nitzotzot) ‚Äî Keep things existing on the manifest level. Every created thing contains these sparks of holiness that sustain its very being.
  • The Life Force (Chiyut) ‚Äî The particular holiness inside ideas and spirit that gives them their individual character.

Concentration and Curvature

When one truly concentrates on the idea that nothing exists besides God, that thought achieves its maximum power. Consider this analogy:

"Just as matter cannot curve space unless it is in a concentrated area, this thought cannot draw the light of God unless one really works at it."

However, the Kabbalists caution: one still needs to channel this light properly into the "vessels" of understanding‚Äîthrough studying works like the Eitz Chayim. Otherwise, the light, being too intense, simply breaks one's vessels. The goal is not merely to draw down light, but to draw it into knowledge that can be contained and integrated.
