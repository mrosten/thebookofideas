The Receding God

Some try to prove God's existence by pointing to what science cannot explain. This approach is fundamentally flawed. What happens when science eventually explains those mysteries? (As it has done countless times before.)

The only robust proofs are those of Spinoza and Descartes‚Äîproofs that rest on logic itself, not on gaps in knowledge.

The Paradox of Divine Retreat

God has removed His light from the Empty Space. The closer we come to proving Him, the further He recedes:

  • Spinoza proves God's existence logically.
  • Then Kant attacks the very ability of logic to prove anything.
  • We reveal His presence, and He withdraws further into the background.

Faith as Foundation

This hiding occurs both in the mental universe of logic and in the physical world, where God sometimes reveals Himself through miracles‚Äîyet withdraws deeper into hiddenness after each revelation.

The only place to truly find God is in one's heart. He reveals Himself inside the hiding place. Only through faith can one leap over the doubts into knowledge.

Rabbi Nachman and the Big Bang

In the Likutey Moharan, Rabbi Nachman of Breslov hints at what modern cosmology calls the Big Bang. He equates the "empty space" (chalal hapanui) with the absence of belief and observes that the more one knows God, the more one realizes how little one knows.

This leads to a profound insight: the spiritual empty space is expanding. If the spiritual realm expands with knowledge, perhaps the physical realm does the same‚Äîan expanding universe, born from an initial point of infinite density and divine withdrawal.
