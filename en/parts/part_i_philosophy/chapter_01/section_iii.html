Kant's Challenge

Kant insisted: Does the mind cause itself? If so, it would be God by Spinoza's own definition. But this appears not to be the case‚Äîor does it?

The resolution lies in understanding that Spinoza's proof doesn't rest on the individual mind, but on Substance itself‚Äîthe totality of existence that contains and transcends all particular minds.
