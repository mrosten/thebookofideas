Divergent Paths in Chassidic Thought

Certain later Chassidic schools established paths that, while valid, differ significantly from early Chasidut. For instance, some deemphasized the central role of the ‚ÄúRebbe,‚Äù which the Baal Shem Tov (Besht) considered essential. Furthermore, the Besht taught that encountering physical beauty (such as a beautiful woman) is an opportunity to elevate that trait to its higher spiritual root‚Äîa practice some later teachers deemed inappropriate for the general populace. Their theological understanding of Divine Oneness also differs. For the Besht, the concept "There is nothing besides God" is literal, similar to the non-dualism of Shankara: everything is God, and there is nothing but His Infinite Light. For some later masters, however, the world exists but is nullified before God "like a ray of light within the sun."
