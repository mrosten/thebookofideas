The Specificity of Ethics

The commandments are not arbitrary commands but logical consequences of spiritual reality. For example, the law against taking a pledge from a widow exists to protect her reputation and privacy. While some authorities argue we cannot limit the scope of the law based on its reason, others point out that the reason is the law. In the Kabbalah, this widow represents the Divine Presence in Exile; protecting her reputation on earth is a way of protecting the higher worlds from desecration.

Cleanliness and Heart

The Rambam summarizes three pillars for the refined person:

  • Reduction of Desire: Moving from animal instinct to Divine purpose.
  • Politeness: The social grease of a holy community.
  • Cleanliness: In heart, action, body, and dress. These are the clothes of the character; without them, the inner light remains unrevealed.
