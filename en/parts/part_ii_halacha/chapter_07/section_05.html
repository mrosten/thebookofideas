The Dangerous Logic of Solomon

"Rabbi Shimon is worthy to be relied upon in a time of pressing need." ‚Äî The Talmudic Safety Net.

King Solomon's audacious decision to marry the daughter of Pharaoh was not a crime of passion, but a calculated act of jurisprudence. He relied on a dangerous but profound principle: Darshinan Taama D'kra‚Äî"We interpret the reason of the verse." Solomon argued that if the Torah forbids a king from having many wives "lest they turn his heart astray," then a king whose heart is essentially unturnable is exempt from the restriction. He applied this same logic to the prohibition of Egypt: if the reason is idolatry, and he converts her, the reason vanishes, and so does the prohibition.

Rabbi Shimon's Axiom: The Reason is the Law Rabbi Shimon Bar Yochai champions the view that the "Why" dictates the "What."
  • The Case of the Widow: The Torah forbids taking a pledge from a widow. Why? To avoid the awkwardness of returning it daily. Rabbi Shimon argues: If she is rich and you don't need to return it daily, the law falls away.
  • The Modern Consequence: This logic suggests that Rabbinical decrees designed to separate Jews from "Idolators" may not apply to modern nations that follow abstract monotheism or secular ethics. If the "fear of idolatry" is gone, the "wall of separation" loses its foundational mortar.

The Reality of the Fence

While the Halacha generally follows the Sages‚Äîwho argue that a law stands even if its reason seems irrelevant‚ÄîRabbi Shimon remains a powerful "minority report" in the system. The Talmud establishes a rule: "Rabbi Shimon is great enough to rely upon in a time of pressure." in our era, where the boundaries between nations are blurring and the "Seven Canaanite Nations" are long gone, the logic of Rabbi Shimon becomes a vital tool.
