This is the halacha to all authorities. Rav Huna held that the holiness of the land of Israel needs to be established by a King and the Urim and Tumim. The halacha is like Rav Huna to all the authorities. Tosphot puts these two together and comes up with the idea that teruma and maaser nowadays is only rabbinical, not biblical. This means in plain english that to this opinion the holiness of the land of Israel nowadays is only rabbinical, not biblical. Thus, even if we concede to the Ramban that there is a biblical mitzvah of living in Israel [which the Rambam doesn't hold with anyway] still who says you can fulfill it nowadays by living in Israel. However, there are opinions that it is Biblically holy also None of these reasons would be sufficient to cause one not to live in Israel except that Jesus said when you see Jerusalem surrounded by armies get up and leave.

This advice saved the original Jewish church in Jerusalem for at first the Roman armies came and surrounded Jerusalem and then the general was called back to Rome. At that point the church remembering Jesus' words got up and left for Pele in Jordan. Then the Roman armies returned and destroyed Jerusalem. Even though Jesus said this then it seems to me that the same advice would apply today. [The idea of Jesus was that Israel doesn't have the light of political stability.
