Chapter 7: Marriage And Family

Section I

Marriage and Family There are five colors of blood that are unclean, and the other colors are pure even if they are red. To the Shulchan Aruch , the Rambam and the Rif and the Rosh and all the major authorities the women of today aren't niddot because they don't have the feeling of the opening of the womb to let out blood. Rav Natrunai Gaon wrote that a nidah needs to go to the mikveh for marriage relations only as a rabbinical decree. It is Biblical only if she goes into the Temple in Jerusalem. So, we have a lot of doubts piling up one on the other all in a case of what the major authorities consider to be not unclean in the first place since there is no feeling of the opening of the womb. That is: m aybe it is a red that is clean. But if you'll say it is a red that is not perhaps the law is like the majority of authorities that without feeling it isn't unclean. But if you'll say the halachah is like the Noda BeYehuda that a feeling of something flowing inside is also considered a feeling, perhaps the law is like Rav Netrunai Gaon.

So, there are a lot of reasons to say that after seven days she is pure immediately without going to the mikvah. But its better to go to a natural body of water or a mikvah if possible. Anyway, to have good children the sex should be only on the Sabbath night (Friday night). After true midnight, after the mikvah. This applies only to when it's possible for children to come from the union. The main thing is for one to attach himself/herself to God while having sex and not to fall into physicality, rather spirituality. [You could say also that she doesn't even have to wait at all because of the first reasons] A man can marry two or more wives.
