Judaism To become Jewish a woman needs only to go to the mikveh for the sake of getting married, or for nida , to clean herself from her cycle. If a non-Jewish woman goes into the mikveh or ocean for the sake of getting married to a Jew or to purify herself she automatically becomes Jewish as Rav Asi said in the Gemarah in Yevamot (the second way is as Rashi explains there). Someone not believing in God, or the Oral and Written Law is not Jewish, no matter if their parents are Jewish.

— Rambam in the Commentary to Sanhedrin The Rambam says in the Commentary on the Mishna in Sanhedrin that one who believes in the 13-Principles-of-Faith is in the category of Israel and it is a mitzva to love him even if he does all the sins he can possibly do. I think the Rambam means that he is in the category of Israel but not actually Jewish until he goes into the mikvah [as per the Talmud and Mishna Torah]. To Rav Sadiah Gaon the main principles are three: God's existence, that he is one, and reward and punishment. To Rav sadiah Gaon it is easy to see that many non Jews can come under the category of Israel.
