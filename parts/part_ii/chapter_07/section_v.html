[Incidentally Rabbi Shimon is the Tana in the Yerushalmi that says Solomon didn't marry her but intended her as a “girlfriend” like Rashi.] Rabbinical law only applies in a place and time that there is a possibility of transgressing the biblical law - this is a general principle throughout the whole Talmud .

Specifically, reading from a light on Yom Kippur is permitted because there is no fear one will come to tilt. Rav sent to his non-Jewish friend in Babylon a present since he knew that he wasn't an idolator and the Rosh and Ritva explain the laws pertaining to non-Jews in general only pertain in places where there are idolators. Thus , since the seven Canaanite nations are no longer common among us [and to the Rambam no longer exist] to the rabbis marrying even an idolator is permitted. To Rabbi Shimon marrying an idolator is forbidden but marrying a non -Jew who isn't an idolator is permitted.

Rabbi Shimon held in general that there is a logical basis for each commandment and in a case where that basis doesn't apply the commandment doesn't apply as in Bava Kama 115a where he says one can take as a pledge the garment of a rich widow since the law of the Torah of not taking the garment of a widow should logically apply only to a poor widow for the reason given there in the Gemara.
