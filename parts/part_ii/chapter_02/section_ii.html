Yet because the Baal Shem Tov in his will did emphasize saying the Shema exactly as the commandment was meant to be I think one should try to do so. [The five things mentioned in the will of the Baal Shem Tov were: a. Learning books of ethics every day. b. Carefulness in keeping Shabbat. c. Carefulness in saying the Shema. d. Going to the mikvah for with the kabalistic intentions. e. Doing a kindness for someone every day.

To Rav Yehuda in the name of Shmuel saying the Shema is a rabbinical mitzva. It seems the authorities hold it is Biblical because of the Gemara in Sucah. However, it would seem that since Brachot is the main place of the subject we ought to follow the conclusion of the Gemara there that it is rabbinical. Grace after bread is possible to shorten. For the first blessing only the first and last sentence are required. [As the Sages said if one says “blessed is the Master of this bread”, he had fulfilled his obligation. However, since a blessing that starts and ends with a blessed can't be changed from that form, one must say as I have said.] For the second one needs to mention only the covenant and the Torah and then the final blessing. For the third one, one needs to mention only the kingdom of David and the last blessing. The fourth needs only the three mentions of goodness and the three mentions of abundance.
