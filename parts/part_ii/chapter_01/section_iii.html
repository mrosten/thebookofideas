The Three Pillars of Law

The Rambam (Maimonides) categorizes the reasons for the commandments into three main areas, aiming to perfect society and the individual soul:

The Structure of Divine Law

The Rambam's Classification (Guide III:31)
  1. Truth & Belief: To teach correct opinions and remove false ones (e.g., Unity of God).
  2. Social Order: To establish a just society and prevent violence (e.g., Do not murder).
  3. Character Refinement: To train the self in good traits and separate from bad habits (e.g., Dietary laws).

God and Logic

There is a debate between Rabbi Shimon and Rabbi Yehuda regarding whether we can derive the scope of a law from its reason (Darshinan Taama D'kra). However, all agree that the laws have reasons. It is not "God vs. Logic"; rather, it is God = Reason. As the Zohar states, "Torah is the spreading out of the Higher Wisdom."

The Social Reason

Example: Taking a pledge from a widow is forbidden because returning it daily would damage her reputation among neighbors. The law protects human dignity.

The Cosmic Reason

Example: The widow represents the Shekhinah (Divine Presence) in exile. Taking a pledge disrupts the flow of light to the lower worlds. The physical reason encompasses the spiritual one.
