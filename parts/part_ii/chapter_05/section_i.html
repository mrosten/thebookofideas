Chapter 5: Kashrut

Section I

The Rashba in a tshuva was asked about a certain food that was specifically mixed with something not Kosher but the amount of the non-Kosher stuff in it was less than [IMAGE: image008.gif] . He wrote that it is permitted. This gets rid of the need for a stamp of Kashrut on a lot of products. The Gemara in Avoda Zara says that the cheese of idolators is permitted and this is brought down as the halachah by the Rif Rambam Tur Shulchan Aruch. Concerning the vessels, it was prepared in, the Rosh in Chulin Chap. 8, Sec. 42 says there is nothing to worry about.

Meat biblically does need to be prepared in a kosher manner. Chicken also needs to be prepared in a kosher manner, but it is only by rabbinical decree.
