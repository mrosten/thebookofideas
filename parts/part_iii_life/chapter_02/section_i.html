Chapter 2: Marriage

Section I

“A hasty attachment is held to be dishonorable, because time is the true test of this as of most other things” -Plato, the Symposium My bounty is as boundless as the sea, My love as deep; the more I give to thee, The more I have, for both are infinite. -Juliet to Romeo I believe in marriage at young ages — in the teenage years as the Talmud says, and as was always done by the disciples of the Baal Shem Tov and the Moroccan Jews, and Yemenite Jews. The sepherah of Foundation is the teenage years and therefore the most appropriate time for procreation, as the Talmud says that Rabbah said, “The reason I am better than my friends is because I married at 16, and if I would have married at 14, I would have said ‘an arrow in the eye of the evil inclination’”, i.e. he wouldn't have been afraid of the evil inclination at all. Also, two wives are permitted even to Ashkenazic Jewry — because the decree of Rabbi Gershon was only for a limited time as it says in the Shulchan Aruch.

One can combine the circular and the straight sephirot into one, which results in the name 63 (since yosher and igulim are from 52 and 45) by considering the combination of a point in the igulim and of a point in yosher as one point in the new type of sephirot of 63 and 72 (as a product manifold) I think this is the spiritual process that we are involved with now for in the redemption there will be the name 72 so we must be in one stage before that.
