The difference between good and evil is that a good person gets pleasure out of giving someone pleasure and an evil person gets pleasure from causing pain. It really has little to do with who is receiving the good for the good, enjoy giving pleasure even to evil people and the evil get pleasure from causing pain even to their friend. Most people are between these two extremes and try to benefit their friends, but this doesn't reflect their true character. The question of how to react to an enemy is the subject of debate in every generation. The Sages of the Talmud say one who is kind in a place where cruelty is needed, will eventually become cruel where kindness is needed. You should pray for the good of your enemies but still guard yourself from them. I think this idea would explain most of the statements of Jesus, the Bible, the Besht, Buddha and the Sutras in this regard, so that there shouldn't be any contradiction.
