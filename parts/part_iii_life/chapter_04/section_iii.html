Section III — The Scale of Governance

The Supreme Individual

The difference between a free society and a tyranny often rests on a slight philosophical pivot. John Locke placed human rights supreme and government secondary, leading to the foundations of the free West. Rousseau, by contrast, made the "General Will" supreme, providing a blueprint for reigns of terror. In the spiritual realm, your inalienable rights are rooted in the Sephirah of Wisdom, which exists above the relative scale of right and wrong.

Inalienable Rights

Rights come from God and are absolute. Government exists only to protect these pre-existing values. It is a system of Correction (Tikun).

Freedom
Collective Command

The majority decides what rights are "permitted." The individual is absorbed into the "General Will." It is a return to the Chaos of Tohu.

Tyranny

Personality as the Door

Everything that happens to a person is an expression of their personality. If you see failure, it is a call to correct a fundamental trait. Happiness cannot be found outside of yourself; it must be created within. The door to a better future is the heart, and talking with God in a private place (Hitbodedut) is the key that unlocks that door.

The Gratitude Foundation While many argue whether the Torah is based on faith or deeds, the Duties of the Heart suggests it is based on Gratitude. Faith is the root, but gratitude is the soil. Evil deeds are often merely evidence that one's inner faith is flawed or purely outward.

The Danger of the Bubble

Isolation is not a cure for chaos. When an individual or community builds a "bubble" to hide from the world, the internal entropy eventually revolts. The higher light only flows through unconstricted vessels; trying to fight the laws of chaos through isolation only results in the chaos overtaking the smaller radius of the bubble even more violently.

“The victory is yours when you yield to your friend.” — Sophocles
