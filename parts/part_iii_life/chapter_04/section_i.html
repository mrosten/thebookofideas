Chapter 4: Ways Of Life

Section I

(or Principles of Life) “One needs no cause. Whatever is decreed to come to one, will come without any cause at all. -Madregas Ha Adam (Rabbi Joseph Yozel Horwitz ) “Not he who fasts and prays is acceptable, rather he who knows HIM. -The Rambam in the Guide for the Perplexed , chapter 53.) One should have absolute trust in God to give him a living and all his needs in spiritual and physical matters. Still, one should do something for a living which is a part of serving God. “Great is work, for God himself has commanded that one should do some minimal amount of work” — Sefer HaMidot (by Rabbi Nachman).

God runs the world between two poles, trust in God and self-reliance: and as with the uncertainty principle the exact location between those two poles you belong at any one instant isn’t determinate. This comes from the dichotomy between the circular and straight sephirot . Rabbi Nachman said one should do just enough effort to make a vessel to receive God's blessing (in a natural way) if he so desires to give it. The idea of the natural vessel is the name Elohim which has the numerical value of the word for nature which reflects the circular sephirot. In that vessel then can come the blessing from the Inflammable Name which reflects the straight sephirot.

In general, the evil inclination can be broken only by an oath to do the right thing. This applies also to any evil habit like masturbation — however its also good not to eat anything in the morning until 12:00 pm, in order to break that evil inclination. Many times, advice you receive in a dream will be much more accurate than even what good friends give. The Rambam held that the statement of the Sages that prophecy was taken from Israel meant only until the year 1240 and that in that year prophecy would return to Israel.
