Section I — The Physics of Trust and Dreams

“One needs no cause. Whatever is decreed to come to one, will come without any cause at all.” — Madregas Ha Adam

The Uncertainty Principle of Trust

God runs the world between two poles: absolute Trust in God and Self-Reliance. Like the uncertainty principle in physics, the exact point where you belong at any given moment is indeterminate. It is a dynamic tension between the Circular Sephirot (Nature/Elohim) and the Straight Sephirot (Infinite Light).

The Circular Vessel

One does the minimal "natural" effort to create a vessel. The name Elohim (Nature) is the boundary; the heart is fixed on the source of the blessing, not the tool.

Infinite Light
The Straight Line

One exerts maximum physical effort, often forgetting the source. While necessary for the "World of Action," it lacks the protective light of the Makif.

Temporal Effort

Prophecy in the Night

Prophecy did not end with the Temple; it shifted into the realm of the unconscious. As the Rambam notes, almost all prophets (except Moses) received their messages while asleep. In our era, a dream is often "more than a dream"—it is the voice of the soul speaking when the static of the ego is silenced.

The Midnight Prayer Dreams can provide specific, daily guidance—identifying a need for cleanliness, a shift in priorities, or a warning of danger. Those who "give up hope" in their own cleverness often find that this is exactly when the Divine Step-In occurs. Hope begins at the point of surrender.
“Not he who fasts and prays is acceptable, rather he who knows HIM.” — The Rambam
