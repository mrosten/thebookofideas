We could use a computer with a statistical package to get the values of the coefficients (even though it's probable that every person would end up having his own coefficients). Also, we could use the t statistic to test the possibility that one of these things has no effect on children. To prove to the doubters, who say that none of these things have an effect we could use the F statistic. You could do this with every chapter in the Lekutai Moharan and thus come out with some results for all the coefficients, which could serve for some practical guidance in ones life. [For it is likely that people’s coefficients don't vary that much one from one another.] Without knowing the value of the coefficients, it’s impossible to know how much each variable effects the situation, and since doing one mitzvah (commandment) invariably takes time and effort from another one, one can verify what he should be doing in order to achieve his goals. (Even though you may have different coefficients than everybody else, it’s unlikely that yours will be significantly different than your immediate peer group, so if you have a general idea of the value of the statistics you can know what you should emphasize without having to waste years experimenting with things that may have little effect on your overall happiness). Even though it is true that much depends on your own trust in God and other variables, so you could ask how is it possible to reduce your personal happiness and success in so many areas as the Lekutai Moharan does to a simple mathematical formula.

The answer is that much of one's own trust in God can be determined by his peer group, for no righteous or great person appears in a vacuum. Baba Sali in Morocco was surrounded by a spirit of fear of God. Spinoza and Rabbi Moshe Chayim Lutzato were deeply affected by the atmosphere of philosophy in Amsterdam (why else would Rabbi Lutzato be the sole saint to create a coherent philosophical analysis of the Kabbalah , very similar to what Spinoza did for philosophy in general). By doing repentance on a sin you get some Divine inspiration. Then, you do repentance again since you realize your first repentance wasn't good enough in accordance with your increased awareness of God's greatness. The second time you repent, your repentance is better so you get a higher degree of inspiration, i.e., inspiration times some number k which is more than one.
