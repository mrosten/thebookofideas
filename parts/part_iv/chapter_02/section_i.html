Chapter 2: Roots Of Science

Section I

Introduction Science and Kabbalah are two distinct fields with apparently no relationship between them. Yet Einstein wrote that the object of all science is to coordinate our experiences and to bring them into a logical system. He also said that there is no real qualitative difference between those sense perceptions which are common to different individuals (and in that sense impersonal) and those which are experienced by only one person. Those which are common to different people are really no more factual than those which are experienced by one. Kabbalah involves personal experience which however can be verified by its effects in the inter-personal world.

It's my hope that this section will coordinate experience (spiritual and scientific) and bring them all into a logical system. It is also my hope to show how Science and Kabbalah can illuminate the other as long as each keeps its respective parameters in mind. When either forgets it's boundaries it usually does notoriously badly. Physics describes reality based on models taken from every day experience and these models work. I am trying here to find what the true inner model of Physics is by using the Kabbalah, i.e., I'm trying to find the spiritual realities upon which the physical world is based.
