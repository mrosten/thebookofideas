It is also interesting to note that Jesus said the same idea as the Rambam in The Guide . Jesus said the Shabbat was given for man. The Rambam wrote that the object of the entire Torah, in general, is the well-being of the body and the soul — and specifically, the Shabbat was given to witness the fact that God created the world, and to give man rest and comfort. [Moreh NevuchimIII:27, 43] Couldn't it have been “ hora’at sha’ah ” [a need of the hour], as the Rambam explains about Eliyahu the prophet on Mount Carmel? For, to the Rambam Eliyahu didn't sacrifice there because he received a prophecy allowing it, rather because of the general principle of “ hora’at sha’ah ”.
