Chapter 3: Jesus And Judaism

Section I

Christians “I testify that whether a man or woman, slave or maid servant, Jew or idol-worshiper, the Divine Presence rests on a person only according to his deeds” -Tana Deve Eliyahu, from Eliyahu the prophet. “For it is known that the souls of Israel are from the earth… but as for the non-Jewish nations whose souls are from Heaven, they need not perform deeds (613 mitzvot .)” -- The Gaon from Vilna (The Gra.) Commentary on Shir HaShirim, Chap. 1, Verse 6. According to Rabbi Shneur Zalman (The Baal HaTanya) (in Parshas VaYishlach) the root of Esav is from worlds higher than Yaakov (Tohu) and that is why Isaac wanted to bless him, but the mistake of Isaac was in not realizing that Esau had fallen from that root.

I think to answer the contradiction between the Gra and the Baal HaTanya that the world the Kings of Edom in the straight sephirot must somehow have a connection with the circular ones. At any rate I didn't know from where the Gra and the Baal Hatanya got this from until I saw in Shaar Ruach HaKodesh: Yichud 10 on the verse, “These are the three Children of Noah” which basically gives as the root of the children of Noah the Three Divine Names of 161, 143, and 151. But the support for the Baal HaTanya’s point of view is the fact that in the same letters are hinted the name Elohim with the yud filling the letters. Even though that is wholly important to note that it is the later permutations of that name which give rise to the Klipot, it seems to me that the root of non-Jews is the three names, “I Will Be” in the eye of Adam Kamdon, not actually the full names of 161 ,143, and 151 respectively, for in the full names there are no circular sephirot, [unless we are referring to the circular sephirot before Adam Kadmon].
