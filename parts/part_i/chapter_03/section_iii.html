Section III â€” The God of Reason

Reason and Revelation Reason (the Mind) and Revelation (the Heart) are the two "universities" of the soul.
  • Reason (Spinoza/Hegel): Deals with the logical proof of God's presence in the physical universe.
  • Revelation (Torah/Prophecy): Deals with the spiritual realm which, while mathematical, transcends logical proof.

Rabbi Nachman and Rambam appear to disagree on which is superior, but they simply operate in different domains. Reason clarifies Revelation, but only Faith can cross the final gap to the Absolute.

The Limits of Logic

Kant argued that logic only proves God in the mind, not in reality. However, if we admit that any law (like Cause and Effect) works in the "real world," we have bridged the gap.

  • Zeno's Paradox: Zeno argued motion is impossible because of infinite divisibility. The answer is that space, like energy, is likely quantized (Planck length). Similarly, the chain of causes cannot be infinite; it must have a discreet First Cause.
  • Strange Loops: God is the "Super-Logic" that completes the system of logic, just as super-natural numbers complete number theory.

The Proofs of God

Spinoza's Propositions Spinoza proves that God is the only Substance.
  1. Prop XIV: Besides God, no substance can be granted or conceived.
  2. Prop XV: Whatsoever is, is in God, and without God nothing can be, or be conceived.

Aristotle's Prime Mover: Since an infinite regression of movers is impossible (like Zeno's paradox), there must be a Prime Mover which moves things by attraction (Love) rather than propulsion.

Hegel's Dialectic: Truth evolves through contradictions (Thesis-Antithesis-Synthesis). This process is like a MÃ¶bius strip that eventually encircles the Absolute.

Spinoza proved God logically. But as Rabbi Nachman hints, the more we prove Him, the more He hides in the "Empty Space" of the spiritual universe.
