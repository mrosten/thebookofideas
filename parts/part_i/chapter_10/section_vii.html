Chapter 10: Gemarah

Section VII

To the Rambam in the Guide [III:51] the division of people is (from highest to lowest): a. The prophets (the Crown). b. The philosophers (Wisdom) [of the schools of Aristotle and Plato, not the rival schools of Greek thought of Epicurus or the several other types that the Sages and the Rambam didn't go for]. c. The scientists (Understanding) (Jewish or not). d. Those who keep the commandments and know the Talmud. (Knowledge-Glory with Royalty). e. Those who keep the commandments (Royalty). f. Those that don't keep the commandments.

The Rambam does stipulate that all should be facing towards the King [the Intellect-God] - otherwise they are evil. Yet, this depends also on why they are turning from the King; if for pleasure then they are from the Klipa of Noga , if for sin they are from the three totally evil klippoth (this corresponds with Aristotle's division of people that believe the purpose of life is to have pleasure), then political people [those that do right for the sake of the right not honor], and those that love and live the contemplative life.

This also corresponds with the three worlds. 1. The pleasure seekers have root - souls from the physical universe.
