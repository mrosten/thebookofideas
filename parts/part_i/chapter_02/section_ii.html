Section II â€” The Structure of Religion

The Y-Axis of Spirituality Visualizing religion on a coordinate system:
  • The Lower 7 Sephirot (Religion): This is the realm of "Religion"â€”rules, rewards, and suffering. It corresponds to the emotional attributes. Here, the battle is between Good and Evil (x-axis).
  • The Upper 3 Sephirot (Light): Above organized religion, the air clears. We are closer to the vertical Light. Here, the battle is not between Good and Evil, but between Truth and Mistake.

This model explains the Rambam's view that Job's suffering stemmed from a lack of knowledge (science/philosophy). Job was righteous (perfect in the Lower 7), but he lacked the higher perspective. Organized religion, by nature, cannot fit every soul perfectly. If applied with rigor, it causes chaos; if applied loosely, it averages out.

Freedom vs. Religion

Values: Freedom

Source: World of Points (Nekudim).

The individual point of existence, focused on autonomy and individual potential.

Values: Order

Source: World of Correction (Tikun/Brudim).

The structured vessel, focused on stability, tradition, and collective discipline.

The ultimate path includes both.

The Trap of Fanaticism

Every religion has two aspects:
  1. Faith (Right Brain): The individual search for God.
  2. Organization (Left Brain): Structure, rules, and politics.

Organized religion often reflects "solidified neurons" that prohibit free thinking. Paradoxically, secular ideologies like Communism or Democracy can become "religions" in this structural sense. But if one learns enough and absorbs it deeply, the force can reach critical mass, breaking through the rigid structures to produce new, living ideas.
