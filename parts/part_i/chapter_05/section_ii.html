Needs vs. Wants

Aristotle held that there is only one "right plan" for a good life: to seek and acquire the things we truly need. Wants are often deceptive, but needs are always good. These include external needs of the body and internal needs of the mind (knowledge) and heart (love and self-esteem).

The Strange Spiral

Happiness is not a destination but a constant movement upward through a hierarchy of needs:

  1. Physical Needs: Food, shelter, and security.
  2. Emotional Needs: To love and be loved.
  3. Mental Needs: Knowledge and the skill of thinking.
  4. Spiritual Needs: Knowledge of God and the infinite soul.

The needs of the body are finite, but the needs of the soul are infinite. Free will is the choice of where on this spiral to work at any given moment.

Tangled Hierarchies

Victor Frankl observed that "giving a man a reason to live" allows him to find the "how." This reason must be the right goal‚Äîthe meeting of internal needs through virtue and habit.
