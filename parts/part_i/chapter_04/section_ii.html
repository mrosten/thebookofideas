Meditation and Hints

God sends His spirit to those willing to obey small hintsâ€”like Moses turning aside to see the burning bush. To receive these hints, one must be "rein-trained" to listen to the softest whisper of the heart.

The Practice of Presence
  • Meditation: Contemplation of the soul (Chovot HaLevavot). Rabbi Nachman describes it as "calling from the depths" without voice or words.
  • Sacred Time: The early Chasidim meditated for hours before and after prayer. Their wisdom was "guarded" and "blessed" within them as a result.
  • Oaths: Making a daily oath to meditate is a powerful tool to turn simple motivation into a sacred duty.

The Physics of Fear

Fear of God is the literal key to wisdom and physical longevity. Studies show that religious commitment significantly lowers rates of heart disease, cancer, and hypertension. This "Awe" acts like an RLC circuit for the mind, enabling it to receive the Infinite Light.

Rambam's Path to Perfection

1. Logic

The foundation of all thinking. Before approaching the Divine, the mind must be corrected and trained in the rules of valid inference.

Foundation
2. Mathematics

Preparing the mind for abstraction. Mathematics moves the observer from the world of senses to the world of pure forms and relationships.

Abstraction
3. Physics

Understanding the physical manifestation of God. The study of the "Works of Creation" (Maaseh Bereshit) reveals the Creator through His acts.

Manifestation
4. Metaphysics

Reaching toward the Divine. The study of the "Works of the Chariot" (Maaseh Merkavah), where philosophy meets the highest spiritual truth.

Transcendence

Community and Character

There is a curious mirror in history: Jewish people are often collectively flawed (due to bad leaders) but individually righteous. Christians are often collectively righteous but individually coarse. This is why Jewish miracles are often private and personal, while others are communal and grand.
