Since Fear of God is such a key factor in wisdom and besides that we have. Rabbi Nachman's way of learning I would like to suggest a way of learning that would result in even a stupid person becoming wise: (a) Learn books of ethics and fear of God every day and keep what they say to do. (b) Learn the subjects you want to learn every day by just saying the words of the book in order from the beginning of the book until the end without skipping or repeats. When you finish the entire book, only then should you repeat the book again. [Sichot Haran 76] This may take years, but I think over the course of much time one will see a greater blessing in his learning than if he had learned in the regularly accepted way.

“Fear of God increases days” Stanislav Kasl of Yale and Ellen Idler of Rutgers, studied 2,812 people in New Haven. They discovered that outwardly religious people, whether Jewish, Protestant or Catholic, were less likely to become medically disabled, than those who didn't attend church or synagogue. A study of 454 men and 85 women in Jerusalem showed that secular [non­religious] Jews had a greater risk of heart disease than Orthodox Jews. Analysts from the University of Florida found that counties in the USA with the highest number of religious people have the lowest rates of cancer. Dr. Larson and his colleagues did a study on 400 men in Georgia, found that people that believed in God and attended religious services had the lowest readings of blood pressure.
