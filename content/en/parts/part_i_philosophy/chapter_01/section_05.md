<h2>Circular and Straight</h2>

<p>The idea that "nothing exists besides God" operates on the level of <em>Sovev Kol Almin</em>—<strong>He surrounds all worlds</strong>. This is the realm of the <strong>Circular Sephirot</strong> (<em>Iggulim</em>): God's infinite light that envelops all creation equally, without distinction.</p>

<p>But there is another mode: <em>Memale Kol Almin</em>—<strong>He fills all worlds</strong>. Here, the <strong>Straight Sephirot</strong> (<em>Yosher</em>) operate: God's life energy is divided and distributed among the different levels of creation in a hierarchical order.</p>

<h3>Sparks and Life Force</h3>

<div class="concept-box">
    <h3>Two Types of Divine Presence</h3>
    <ul class="clean-list">
        <li><strong>The Sparks</strong> (<em>Nitzotzot</em>) — Keep things existing on the manifest level. Every created thing contains these sparks of holiness that sustain its very being.</li>
        <li><strong>The Life Force</strong> (<em>Chiyut</em>) — The particular holiness inside ideas and spirit that gives them their individual character.</li>
    </ul>
</div>

<h3>Concentration and Curvature</h3>

<p>When one truly concentrates on the idea that nothing exists besides God, that thought achieves its maximum power. Consider this analogy:</p>

<blockquote class="fancy-quote">
    "Just as matter cannot curve space unless it is in a concentrated area, this thought cannot draw the light of God unless one really works at it."
</blockquote>

<p>However, the Kabbalists caution: one still needs to channel this light properly into the "vessels" of understanding—through studying works like the <em>Eitz Chayim</em>. Otherwise, the light, being too intense, simply breaks one's vessels. The goal is not merely to draw down light, but to draw it <em>into knowledge</em> that can be contained and integrated.</p>
