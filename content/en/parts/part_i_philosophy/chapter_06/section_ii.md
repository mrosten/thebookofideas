            <h4>Collective vs. Individual Reward</h4>
            <p>When the Torah guarantees rain and prosperity for keeping the mitzvot, it is speaking to the <strong>Plural</strong>—the Jewish people as a collective. If 51% of the people are good, the land as a whole prospers. Individually, however, the wicked may still hold the high offices and wealth, while the righteous are preserved in a lowly way until their "horn is raised."</p>
            <div class="concept-box">
            <strong>The Three Storehouses of Reward</strong>
            The afterlife is tiered according to the nature of one's service:
            <ol>
            <li><strong>Lower Gan Eden:</strong> For those whose physical deeds were righteous.</li>
            <li><strong>Middle Gan Eden:</strong> For those who guarded their speech and spoke words of truth.</li>
            <li><strong>Higher Gan Eden:</strong> For those whose inner thoughts and feelings were integrated into the Light.</li>
            </ol>
            </div>
            <p>Ultimately, God's justice is not a simple "if-then" statement, but a deep sifting of every level of human consciousness.</p>
