            <h4>Needs vs. Wants</h4>
            <p>Aristotle held that there is only one "right plan" for a good life: to seek and acquire the things we truly <strong>need</strong>. Wants are often deceptive, but needs are always good. These include external needs of the body and internal needs of the mind (knowledge) and heart (love and self-esteem).</p>

            <p>Victor Frankl observed that "giving a man a <strong>reason</strong> to live" allows him to find the "how." This reason must be the right goal‚Äîthe meeting of internal needs through virtue and habit.</p>
            <div class="concept-box">
            <strong>G√∂del and the Loop</strong>
            Any system that refers to itself (like the human mind) must be incomplete. This <strong>Strange Loop</strong> is not a flaw, but a driver toward higher levels of existence. As Hofstadter pointed out, the mind is a "Tangled Hierarchy" where causes propagate both upward and downward.
            </div>
            <p>The Torah provides the "good habits" and virtues needed to navigate this spiral without having to resolve every dilemma from scratch. It is the roadmap for the mind to reach the Absolute.</p>
