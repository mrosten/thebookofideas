            To maintain a balanced spiritual diet, one should ideally ingest "one class" from each of the main four categories daily. For instance:
            <ol>
            <li><strong>Bible</strong> (Written Law)</li>
            <li><strong>Mishna/Halacha</strong> (Codified Law)</li>
            <li><strong>Talmud</strong> or <strong>Science</strong> (The "Give and Take" of Wisdom)</li>
            <li><strong>Kabbalah</strong> (The Secrets)</li>
            </ol>
            On the level of <strong>Zeir Anpin</strong> (the specific emotional attributes of God, often associated with "Beauty"), we find a library of works that balance the intellect and the heart. The books which emanate from the <strong>Beauty of Atzilut</strong> (Emanation) include:
            <ul>
            <li><strong>Crown:</strong> <em>Sipurai Maasiot</em> (Rabbi Nachman's Stories) - The highest, simplest, yet deepest level.</li>
            <li><strong>Wisdom:</strong> <em>Chassidic Musar Works</em> - The intellectual approach to Divine service.</li>
            <li><strong>Understanding:</strong> <em>Noam Elimelech</em> - The emotional depth of the righteous.</li>
            <li><strong>Malchut & The Six Attributes:</strong> <em>Likutai Moharan</em> and <em>Sefer HaMidot</em>.</li>
            </ul>
            <p>(Note: This classification aligns with the Rambam and Chovot Halevavot's view of serving God through refined intellect and character.) ‚Ä†</p>

            <div class="concept-box">
            <strong>Sephirot Mapping: The Prophets and Writings</strong>
            The diagram above details the "physicality" of the Sephirah of Beauty (Zeir Anpin). In this intricate structure, every letter corresponds to a channel of Divine influence:
            <ul>
            <li><strong>c (Crown):</strong> The root of the Five Books of Moses.</li>
            <li><strong>w, u, k (Wisdom, Understanding, Kindness):</strong> The roots of the Prophets (<em>Nevi'im</em>). Specifically, the Right and Left columns hold the prophets.</li>
            <li><strong>p, b, v (Power, Beauty, Victory):</strong> The Middle Column holds the roots of the Sacred Writings (<em>Ketuvim</em>), such as Psalms and Proverbs.</li>
            </ul>
            </div>
            The next diagram illustrates the world of <strong>Formation (Yetzirah)</strong>, which corresponds to the Mishna. The six orders of the Mishna map directly to the lower sephirot of this world.
            <div class="feature-image" style="width: 80%; margin: 1.5rem auto;">
            <img src="../../../../images/" alt="Book Image" style="width: 100%; height: auto;">
            </div>
            <ul>
            <li><strong>Seeds, Times, Women:</strong> The middle three attributes [Power, Beauty, Kindness] are the root of these first three orders.</li>
            <li><strong>Damages, Sacrifices, Purity:</strong> The lower three attributes [Victory, Splendor, Foundation] form the root of the latter three.</li>
            </ul>
            <p>This means that study is an act of spiritual engineering. By learning the specific tractates, one draws specific Divine Names into their corresponding vessels:</p>
            <ul>
            <li><strong>Seeds (Zeraim):</strong> Draws the light of the name <strong>mlp la pla</strong> from the upper vessels into <strong>Kindness</strong>.</li>
            <li><strong>Times (Moed):</strong> Draws the light of <strong>myhla</strong> (Elohim) into <strong>Power</strong>.</li>
            <li><strong>Women (Nashim):</strong> Draws the light of <strong>tvabe</strong> into <strong>Beauty</strong>.</li>
            <li><strong>Damages (Nezikin):</strong> Draws the light of <strong>laa</strong> into <strong>Victory</strong>.</li>
            <li><strong>Sacrifices (Kodshim):</strong> Draws the complex light of <strong>myhla yhla hla laa</strong> into <strong>Splendor</strong>.</li>
            <li><strong>Purity (Taharot):</strong> Draws the light of <strong>yds</strong> (Shaddai) into <strong>Foundation</strong>.</li>
            </ul>

            Understanding this, we see that the Talmud is not monolithic. The "Talmud" we typically study is often just the <em>Talmud of the Nefesh</em> (the lowest level of soul). This explains why it can sometimes feel dry or disconnected. The goal is to ascend the ladder. The Talmud is incomplete because the souls of many of the Amoraim (Talmudic Sages) were rooted in <strong>Beauty of Emanation</strong>, but the text we have has descended.
