            <h4>The Divine Spark in the Physical</h4>
            <p>One of the theological hurdles regarding the Trinity is the assertion that God cannot descend into a physical body. Yet, the Gemara (<em>Sanhedrin 95b</em>) presents a startling narrative regarding Sancheriv. Rabbi Abahu states: "If the verse didn't write it, it would be impossible to say." The text implies that the Holy One, Blessed is He, appeared as an old man to give Sancheriv a haircut, leading to a spark singeing his beard.</p>
            
            <p>Rabbi Abahu emphasizes that this is not a mere parable; the language used—<em>"The Holy One, Blessed is He"</em>—typically refers to the Sephira of <em>Tiferet</em> (Beauty/Truth). This implies a direct manifestation of the Divine into the physical realm, a concept echoed in the Patriarchal narratives.</p>
