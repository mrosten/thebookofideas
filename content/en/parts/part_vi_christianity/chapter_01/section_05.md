When the "Straight Sephirot" (the specific revelation of Torah) were later emanated, they did not simply overlap; rather, the path to reconnect them passes through a singularity.</p>

            <p>From the perspective of the Straight Sephirot, this connection point looks like a "Black Hole"—the lowest depth one could sink to, a place where one appears to remain forever. Yet, this singularity is the wormhole between worlds. To travel from the enclosed system of the Law to the open system of the Nations, one must pass through this zero-point.             <h4>The World Line of the Tzadik</h4>
            <p>The concept of the Divine manifesting within a specific spacetime coordinate is not entirely foreign to the tradition. The Gemara (Sanhedrin) mentions a provocative aggadah where God "gave a haircut" to Nebuchadnezzar, implying a manifestation in a physical form to interact with history. If we view the Soul of a Saint (Tzadik) not as a static point but as a continuous "World Line" stretching through the higher dimensions, we resolve many paradoxes.</p>

            <p>At any single slice of time, the soul of a Tzadik might appear "Good" (aligned with the arrow of time) or "Bad" (an anti-particle moving backward). As the Zohar states, "The spirit of Moses is stretched out throughout all generations." To the Ramchal, this generic existence is the "Time-Line" itself. Thus, the figure of Jesus can be understood as a specific intersection of this World Line—a connection to the Sephira of Foundation (<em>Yesod</em>) which holds the Light of Kindness (<em>Chesed</em>).</p>

            <h4>The Lineage Paradox</h4>
            <p>Since Jesus is unified with the attribute of Foundation, he serves as the conduit implies a pre-Messianic role. It is impossible to receive this specific "Light of Kindness" without some connection to this pipeline. However, the Halacha demands that the final Messiah (Son of David) must come from the male seed of David. Jesus, by his own followers' admission, was descended from David only through his mother (or via adoption), which technically disqualifies him from the <em>legal</em> throne of David but perfectly positions him for the <em>spiritual</em> role of Joseph—the one who feeds the nations before the final redemption.</p>
